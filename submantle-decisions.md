- **2026-03-10** | Open access + earned trust tiers (credit score model) | Validators flagged missing auth as a blocker. GL reframed: anonymous access IS the design. Agents don't have to identify themselves for basic queries. Trust is earned through registration and behavior — unlocking better rates, deeper features, premium access. Three tiers: Anonymous (full rates, basic access), Registered (Substrate Verified, better rates, history tracking), Trusted (best rates, premium features, priority). Beta Reputation System scores the journey. Revenue lever: agents are incentivized to register and behave well because it saves them money. Trust as market force, not gate.
- **2026-03-11** | Always aware never acting applies to trust layer — Substrate only exposes trust scores, never blocks/gates/throttles agents | Three validators independently flagged trust enforcement (blocking, rate gating, throttling) as violating the founding principle. Resolution: Substrate provides scores, brands/platforms enforce their own thresholds. This makes Substrate infrastructure (Visa model) not a gatekeeper, creates more revenue opportunities (every consumer needs their own enforcement layer), and avoids regulatory complications.
- **2026-03-11** | SD-JWT (RFC 9901) for V1 trust attestations, not BBS+ | BBS+ cryptosuites are still W3C Candidate Recommendation (not finalized), no Go library support, depends on IETF draft with no timeline. SD-JWT is a finalized RFC (November 2025), has Go libraries, and the EU Digital Identity Wallet ecosystem converged on it. BBS+ deferred to V2 roadmap.
- **2026-03-11** | Pure Beta formula for V1 trust scoring — no temporal weighting, no multi-signal composite | Team 5 proposed three-signal composite (temporal + behavioral + operational). All three validators flagged as over-engineering. Team 3s pure Beta (trust = total_queries / (total_queries + incidents), initialized at 1,1 = 0.5) is validated by academic literature and three independent research passes. Multi-signal scoring deferred to V2 with real baseline data.
- **2026-03-11** | Deterministic scoring only — no ML-based anomaly detection or pattern inference | EU AI Act April 2025 guidance excludes systems based on rules defined solely by natural persons. Substrate Beta formula is deterministic arithmetic. Adding ML would potentially enter EU AI Act high-risk scope. This is both a regulatory and design principle: stay deterministic, stay exempt.
- **2026-03-11** | Agent identity = registered software entity, not model, not context window, not user | The model is an interchangeable engine (employee). The context window is a temporary session (phone call). The user is the human customer. Trust scores belong to the registered software entity (the company). This means trust survives model changes, accumulates across sessions, is portable, and belongs to the builder.
